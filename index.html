The Morality of Abortion and a Potential Middle Ground

An Introduction

It's no question that there's a lot of controversial opinions regarding abortion, and the morality of it. It's something that sparks debate from both sides. On one hand, we have pro-life people. Their belief advocates for the survival of any pregnancy, usually under any circumstance. On the other side, we have pro-choice people. Their belief usually consists of the saying "my body my choice". They widely believe the decision to keep, or to abort a child is completely up to the mother. Because of these vastly differing opinions, it is often hard to find a middle ground. This website is to discuss, view the opposing sides, and most importantly, come to a middle ground in this conversation. I think it's important we try to come together and understand why someone might have an opposing opinion. The only way we can grow as a society is to have these meaningful discussions regardless of the topic.

Pro Life

The pro-life perspective mainly centers their belief that all life has value regardless of what stage of life they are at. Any child is worthy of dignity and worth, even if unborn. They argue it is morally wrong to intentionally take the life of an innocent child, regardless of the circumstances. There are also some who argue for the concern of the woman's well-being when going through the abortion process. Some believe abortion should not be a solution to an unwanted pregnancy, as it can have long-term damaging effects on the mother's physical and emotional health. Another major factor of being pro-life is the belief in religion. Many pro-life people base their stance based off of their religious upbringing which emphasize the importance of protecting the unborn. Pro Choice

When it comes to pro-choice believers, they advocate for autonomy and bodily integrity. There is the vast belief that women should be entitled to their own bodies, and have the choice to do what they'd like with it. Opposing the view of pro-life believers, they believe there is religious freedom, meaning they do not have to follow pro-lifer's beliefs and customs. Pro-choice people often advocate for preventative pregnancy and education. Meaning having a better access to education and healthcare. They argue that by providing accurate information, and access to affordable contraception, could reduce the amount of unintended pregnancies as well as the need for abortions. The Potential Middle Ground and Conclusion

While it may often be difficult to sway someone's beliefs, that might not always need to be the goal for some. In the article Cousins Divided on Abortion Find Ways to Support Each Other, Kayla Meyers and Tori LaBelle have different opinions regarding abortion. Meyers and LaBelle grew up in similar households. They're cousins who were brought up religiously, believing abortion is wrong under any circumstances. That was until Meyers started attending college and had a change of opinion. She had a child and went through severe postpartum depression. She understood motherhood is not for everyone, and it can often be a challenge to some to carry out a pregnancy. LaBelle on the other hand, remained firm with her position that abortion is wrong, but she doesn't fault Meyers for it. They have been able to come together and realize they can still love each other regardless of opinion. They've been able to come to a middle ground. Affordable healthcare, affordable childcare, paid parental leave for mothers and fathers, government programs and non profit support are all resources that can help mother's. It's not easy to have a child in this economy by any means, which often leads to abortion. My middle ground is, if you are religious, there is nothing wrong with following your beliefs. There is also no reason or validity to shame another person for not sharing that belief. For the people rallying outside of clinics that carry out abortions, find something better to do with your time. Support the non profit organizations, adopt a child in the foster care system, or even better, personally help the mother who's abortion you are trying to prevent instead of shaming them. We don't know anyone's personal situation other than our own, so it's best to keep an open mind.
